The Mighty Resurrection


The spirit and resilience of New York were tested. From the ashes of original World Trade Center rose the patriotic 1776 feet Freedom Tower. The architect was David Childs, of the architectural firm Skidmore, Owings & Merrill.

It is now officially known as One World Trade Center, currently the tallest building in the Western Hemisphere, and the sixth-tallest in the world.

On the 100th. floor at the One World Observatory, one can explore the expansive 360 degree panoramic view of New York City and points beyond.


Westfield and Path Station inside the Oculus


This is the interior of the Oculus, designed by architect Santiago Calatrava.

It is the state-of-the-art World Trade Center Transportation Hub. Completed in 2016, it houses the Westfield World Trade Center and the World Trade Center Path Station. It is a centerpiece that also incorporates 78,000 square feet of multi level state-of-the-art retail and dining.


World Trade Center Memorial and Museum


On September 11, 2011, a dedication ceremony commemorating the tenth anniversary of the attacks was held at the memorial and it opened to the public the following day. The museum was dedicated on May 15, 2014 and opened on May 21. Three months after its opening, the memorial had been visited by over a million people.

The September 11 Museum was dedicated on May 15, 2014 and opened to the public on May 21. The exhibits include 23,000 images, 10,300 artifacts, nearly 2,000 oral histories of those killed, mostly provided by friends and families, and over 500 hours of video.


One World Trade Center


The supertall structure has the same name as the North Tower of the original World Trade Center. It is the main building of the rebuilt World Trade Center complex, standing on the northwest corner, bounded by West Street to the west, Vesey Street to the north, Fulton Street to the south, and Washington Street to the east.

Together with the many new luxury residential towers and highrises as a result of the current real-estate boom, this new skyscraper has greatly and significantly changed the skyline of not only Lower Manhattan but New York as well.

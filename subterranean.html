HOME THEN NOW SUBTERRANEAN

State of the Art Subterrranean




The East River waterfront to the Hudson River waterfront; sheltered from the elements guide with dining, shopping, comfort and visual marvels. A point to point exciting exploration.

Historically rich Lower Manhattan has a very extensive subterranean network that includes vast subway tunnels; operating and abandoned subway stations; and extensive pedestrian passageways that almost stretches from the East River waterfront, at Fulton and Williams Street; to the Hudson River waterfront. Lower Manhattan now has a 21st. century state of the art subterranean that links two major transportation hubs, two major shopping centers and stretches all the way to the Freedom Tower and through to the Hudson River waterfront.



Dining Destination


Before heading down to the subterranean levels, it is worth noting that the heart of Lower Manhattan has now been solidified as a dining destination with Chef Nobuyuki Matsuhisa's Nobu Downtown. The newly opened restaurant is in the landmarked former AT&T headquarters; on the block between Fulton Center and the 9/11 Memorial and Museum. The visually stunning modernity decor that merges two unique cultures with the building's stately columns makes the sets of Mad Men looks like a playhouses.


Fulton Center



The Fulton Center is a major transit hub and retail complex at the intersection of Broadway and Fulton Street. It is a glass and steel structure that has a very science fiction design, resembling the interior of a movie spaceship with huge screens of flashing advertisements. The retailers here mostly cater to commuters with a New York's favorite, Shake Shake as the anchor tenant. Reference the first image to the left for all the subway lines at this station.


The circular designs leading to the subway entrances and platforms bear similarites to a coliseum. It is the starting point of the state-of-the-art subterranean section of Lower Manhattan.

On the second lower level of the Fulton Center, a new brightly lit 40 foot wide underground passageway named Dey Street Passageway runs an entire city block to connect Fulton Center to the Oculus.

Westfield World Trade Center


The west end of the Dey Street Passageway opens into the Oculus with a breathtaking vision in white. The vast expanse design seems to be part cathedral, part futuristic movie set, (part my home... oh! no, I was daydreaming for a moment), with the atmospherics of a large stadium. The opennes of the concourse allows one to walk, stride, glide, stroll and marvel freely. The ribbed structured glass ceiling brings sunlight into the subterranean levels and also allows segmented views to the sky and buildings around. The exterior of the Oculus bears features of "a bird released from hand."


This vision of marvel houses the WTC Path Station and the Westfield World Trade Center, a major shopping center of all categories from candy to electronics. The Apple Store is located in the North Concourse and Eataly, the Italian gourmet marketplace in the South Concourse.

At the west side of the Oculus, the entrance to the WTC Path Station bears another marvelous vision in white; resembling a grand emperor's throne with futuristic flairs. Just north of the station, the long, clean and bright white passageway at the West Concourse leads to escalators to the lobby of One World Trade Center. Here one can choose an upward detour; from subterranean to the sky.



One World Observatory



Located on the 100th. floor of One World Trade Center, this observatory welcomes the world with its Sky Pod Elevator to the expansive panoramic view of the entire city and perhaps forever on a clear day. Experience hovering at 100 story in the sky by standing over the glass Sky Portal disc. There are 3 dining options with distinct flavors and unparalleled views on the 101st. floor. Advanced ticket can be purchased at One World Observatory.

The passageway at the West Concourse merge into the beige wide underpass under the high traffic West Street to the long escalators into Brookfield Place. This luxury shopping center offers well known names from Burberry to Zegna and Equinox Fitness to boot.


Brookfield Place


At the Winter Garden Atrium, views of the Hudson River waterfront can be captured without stepping outside. Le District, a French marletpalce and restaurant, is an ideal choice to refuel and restore from shoppping to almost dropping. Another great alternative is Hudson Eats, a sleek dining court that offers fast-casual concept dining from 14 chef-driven eateries; offering wide selections from barbecues to sushi.
Hudson River Park and waterfront is just right outside for all to enjoy. At this point, one can choose to take the ferry to Jersey City or backtrack to Fulton Center and enjoy the tour again in the opposite direction.

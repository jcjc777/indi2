HOME THEN NOW SUBTERRANEAN

State of the Art Subterrranean




The East River waterfront to the Hudson River waterfront; sheltered from the elements guide with dining, shopping, comfort and visual marvels. A point to point exciting exploration.

Lower Manhattan has a very extensive subterranean network that includes vast subway tunnels, operating and abandoned subway stations, and extensive pedestrian passageways that almost stretches from the East River waterfront, at Fulton and Williams Street, to the Hudson River waterfront. Lower Manhattan now has a 21st. century state of the art subterranean that links two major transportation hubs and two major shopping center and stretches all the way to the Freedom Tower and through to the Hudson River waterfront.



Dining Destination


Before heading down to the subterranean levels, it is worth noting that the heart of Lower Manhattan has now been solidfied as a dining destination with Chef Nobuyuki Matsuhisa's Nobu Downtown. The newly opened restaurant is at the landmarked former AT&T headquarters; on the block between Fulton Center and the 9/11 Memorial and Museum. The visually stunning modernity decor of that merges two unique cultures together with the building's stately columns makes the set of Mad Men looks like a playhouse.


Fulton Center



The Fulton Center is a major transit hub and retail complex at the intersection of Broadway and Fulton Street. It is a glass and steel structure has a very science fiction design, resembling the interior of a movie spaceship with huge screens of flashing advertisements. The retailers mostly cater to commuters with a New York's favorite, Shake Shake as the anchor tenant.


The circle designs leading to the subway entrances and platforms bear similarites to a coliseum. It is the starting point of the state-of-the-art subterranean section of Lower Manhattan.

On the second lower level of the Fulton Center, a new brightly lit 40 foot wide underground passageway named Dey Street Passageway runs an entire city block to connect Fulton Center to the Oculus.

Westfield World Trade Center


The west end of the Dey Street passageway opens into the Oculus with a breathtaking vision of white. The vast expanse design seems to be part chathedral, part futuristic movie set, (part my home, oh! no, I was daydreaming for a moment), with features of "a bird released from hand." The ribbed structured glass ceiling brings sunlight into the subterranean levels and also to look out with segmented view to the sky and buildings around.


This vision of marvel also houses the WTC Path Station and the Westfield World Trade Center, a major shopping center of all categories from candy to electronics. The Apple Store is located in the North Concourse and Eataly in the South Concourse.


One World Observatory



At the west side of the Oculus, the entrance to the WTC Path Station bears another marvelous visual in white, resembling a grand emperor's throne with futuristic flairs. Just north of this station a long clean and bright white passageway at the West concourse that leads to escalators into the lobby of One World Trade Center.



The passageway at the West Concourse connects to a wide underpass to long escalators into Brookfield Place.



Brookfield Place


Many luxury brands are available at Brookfield Place. Here one can shop till one drops before venturing out to the Hudson River waterfront or a ferry to Jersey City.
